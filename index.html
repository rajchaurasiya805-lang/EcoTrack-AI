Step 1: Weekly Commute & Transportation

Tell us how you get around on a weekly basis. Estimates are perfectly fine.

Cycling and walking are zero carbon! We use this to track your active metrics.

Step 2: Household Energy & Electricity

Enter your monthly utilities usage. This represents the emissions associated with heating, cooling, and powering your home.

Average single household consumes roughly 150-300 kWh monthly.

Step 3: Food Habits & Diet

Agriculture, livestock farming, and logistics contribute heavily to global emissions. Select your general dietary style.

Vegan

Entirely plant-based diet. Lowest carbon footprint due to absence of meat and dairy.

~1.5 Tons CO₂e/yr

Vegetarian

Plant-based diet but includes dairy and eggs. Moderate footprint due to dairy processing emissions.

~1.7 Tons CO₂e/yr

Low Meat / Flexitarian

Primarily vegetarian diet, but occasionally consumes poultry or fish. Moderately high carbon footprint.

~2.5 Tons CO₂e/yr

High Meat Consumer

Frequent consumer of red meat (beef, pork, lamb). Highest carbon intensity due to livestock methane.

~3.3 Tons CO₂e/yr
